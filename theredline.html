The Redline

Over the course of a year, I led a small team of computer science students to create a product for an outside client for our Junior Capstone. We worked with John Pecak to create "The RedLine", a mobile application for both Android and iOS that allows car enthusiast to get connected with their communities and RSVP to car show events and meetups. I was the project lead for my team and worked as a manager, designer, and programmer while also maintaining client communications. Our application supports two users (host or attendees), which is selected when users sign up for an account with our app.

Our team used React Native and Firebase's Firestore to build our application. We used Expo Go to simulate our application and demo the app for our client and class. I mainly focused on the frontend implementation and interactive design thinking for our team in terms of programming. I've linked a slideshow below that highlights the main features of our application. These features include: an interactive Google API map, a filtering feature, event creation, and event editing.



During the first 6 months of this project, we focused preparing the design documentation and improving our technical writing skills. This included creating our vision statement, client charter, user research, user stories, and etc. After finishing our technical documents, we created our initial prototype using Figma, which our client gave feedback and approval to.

During the second semester of our project, we implemented our Figma designs for the mobile app. We worked in close relations with our client and met with him on a bi-weekly basis to demonstrate our progress and also receive feedback on the features being implemented.
