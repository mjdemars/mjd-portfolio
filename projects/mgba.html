About Me

Foxtrot

For my class Media Devices and Architecture, our final project was to build a GBA game from start to finish. All assets in my game (including: backgrounds, sprites, music, sounds, etc.) were made by myself. The game itself was coded in C and emulated through mGBA and includes features such as parallax scrolling backgrounds, collision maps, etc. In my game, you are tasked with helping a nine-tailed fox to get its fox bead back. You battle evil spirits in battle and have to defeat 3 in order to get to the final boss battle. You also have the chance to stock up on heals to be used in battle by doing minigames scattered through the map. A short play through of my game is linked below to demonstrate some of the features.


Through this project, I deepened my knowledge of what it takes to make a game from start to finish. Of course, my game is on the small scale since it was independently made within the span of a month, but I was able to improve my skills as a programmer, planner, and artist. One of my personal favorite things about playing games is the sound design, so I had the utmost fun composing and recording all the music/sound effects for my game as well.
